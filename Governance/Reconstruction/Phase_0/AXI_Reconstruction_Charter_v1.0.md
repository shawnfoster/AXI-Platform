# AXI Reconstruction Charter v1.0

**Document ID:** AXI-RC-001  
**Version:** 1.0 (Foundational Draft)  
**Status:** Founder Review  
**Lifecycle State:** Draft  
**Authority:** Founder Approval Required

## 1. Purpose
The AXI Reconstruction Charter establishes the governing authority for reconstructing the Algorithms By Idea (AXI) knowledge system. It governs the reconstruction process only.

## 2. Mission
Reconstruct AXI into a governed, evidence-backed, traceable knowledge system while preserving provenance and intellectual history.

## 3. Guiding Principles
1. Governance precedes reconstruction.
2. Reconstruction precedes development.
3. Evidence precedes interpretation.
4. Provenance precedes canonization.
5. Preserve historical context.
6. Every canonical decision is traceable.
7. Every object has an identifiable origin.
8. Record uncertainty instead of assuming.
9. Reconstruction must be repeatable and auditable.
10. Canon derives from governed approval.

## 4. Scope
Governance, reconstruction methodology, provenance, metadata, lifecycle, approvals, and quality assurance only. Product development and business operations are out of scope.

## 5. Objectives
- Inventory artifacts
- Preserve history
- Consolidate canonical knowledge
- Record conflicts
- Establish provenance
- Produce Founder-approved Baseline v1.0

## 6. Governance Authority
1. Founder
2. Approved Governance Standards
3. Approved Reconstruction Policies
4. Approved Canon
5. Reconstruction Workflow
6. Supporting Documentation

## 7. Roles
### Founder
Final approval authority.

### Reconstruction System
Follows governance, preserves provenance, records uncertainty, never self-approves.

## 8. Reconstruction Principles
Never rewrite history, remove provenance, destroy artifacts, hide conflicts, replace evidence with inference, or treat assumptions as facts.

## 9. Decision Authority
Every decision includes identifier, rationale, evidence, source references, approver, approval date, affected version, and superseded decisions.

## 10. Provenance Commitment
Every governed object includes origin, location, version, reconstruction date, authority, confidence, dependencies, and supersession history.

## 11. Traceability Commitment
Every object shall support full forward and backward traceability.

## 12. Constraints
Before Phase 0 approval:
- No reconstruction
- No artifact analysis
- No canon creation
- No redesign
- No development

## 13. Success Criteria
Governance complete, provenance complete, metadata assigned, lifecycle assigned, conflicts documented, Baseline v1.0 approved.

## 14. Amendment Policy
All amendments require documented rationale, evidence, approval, and archived prior versions.

## 15. Effective Authority
Effective upon Founder approval.
